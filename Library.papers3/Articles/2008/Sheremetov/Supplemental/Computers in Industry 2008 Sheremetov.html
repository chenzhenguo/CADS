    Computers in Industry

    Volume 59, Issue 9, December 2008, Pages 936-948

    Knowledge-based collaborative engineering of pipe networks in the upstream and downstream petroleum industry

    Abstract

    This paper describes an approach to the development of a framework for the automation of the detailed engineering cycle based on the integration of concurrent and collaborative engineering models with knowledge-based engineering. The framework is illustrated by the example of pipe networks design and stress and flexibility analysis in the upstream or downstream petroleum industry. Engineering tools are integrated with a Multi-Agent System for helping stress analysis engineers to identify whether a given pipe arrangement can cope with weight, thermal and pressure stress at safe operation levels and suggesting available solutions on pipeline layout and support distribution based on historical cases. Overall, the system architecture is discussed and implementation details are provided.

    Vitae

    Leonid Borisovitch Sheremetov graduated (with honours) from St. Petersburg State Marine Technical University (former Leningrad Shipbuilding Institute), Russia in 1982. The same year he joined St. Petersburg Institute for Informatics and Automation of the Russian Academy of Sciences were he obtained his Ph.D. in computer science in 1990. Currently he is a Principal Investigator of the Research Program in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science of the Mexican Petroleum Institute (IMP). He has led a number of projects for the energy industry with special focus on industrial exploitation of computational intelligence and agent technologies. His research interests include Multi-Agent Systems, Hybrid Intelligent Systems, Data Mining, and Semantic Service Oriented Architectures. He has published more than 200 refereed technical papers in reputable journals, conferences and book chapters. In 2007 he was awarded the Annual IMP Award in Research. He serves as Associate Editor of the IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man and Cybernetics, Part C and of the Int. Journal Computer Science and Systems. Served as a Guest Editor of several journals. He is member of the Technical Committee on Computer Supported Cooperative Work in Design of the IEEE Systems, Man and Cybernetics Society and member of the Technical Committee on Artificial Intelligence and Expert Systems of the IASTED.

    Ildar Zakirzjanovich Batyrshin (M.S., Moscow Physical-Technical Institute, 1975; Ph.D., Moscow Power Engineering Institute, 1983; Dr.Sci., Highest Attestation Committee of Russia, 1996) joined the Department of Informatics and Applied Mathematics at the Kazan State Technological University, Tatarstan, Russia, in 1975, and served as its chairman and full professor from 1997 to 2003. Since 1999, he has also been with the Institute of Problems of Informatics of Academy of Sciences of Tatarstan, as a Leading Researcher. Since 2003 he is a Researcher of Research Program in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science in Mexican Petroleum Institute. His areas of research are Soft Computing, Data Mining, Decision-Making and Expert Systems. He is a co-author and co-editor of several books and special journal issues on Soft Computing and Data Mining published in Russian and English. He was awarded the State Scientific Scholarship, given by the Presidium of Russian Academy of Sciences in 1997 and 2000, the title Honored Scientist of the Republic of Tatarstan, Russia, in 2002. In 2007 he was awarded the Annual IMP Award in Research. He has held long-term visiting research positions at various institutions in Germany, Turkey, Hungary, Italy and Czech Republic. Currently he is a Secretary of the Society of Mathematics of Uncertainty, a member of Council of International Fuzzy Systems Association (IFSA) Society and Past-President of the Russian Association for Fuzzy Systems and Soft Computing.

    Manuel Chi Chim graduated from Mathematics Faculty of Yucatan University (Mexico). Currently he is about to get a M.S. degree in Computer Science at the National Polytechnic Institute of Mexico. Currently he is a research assistant of the Research Program in Applied Mathematics and Computer Science of the Mexican Petroleum Institute. His research interests include Service Oriented Architectures, Distributed Systems, Software Security and Multi-Agent Systems.

    Armando Rosas Elguera received the Diploma in Civil Engineering from National Polytechnic Institute of Mexico. He joined Mexican Petroleum Institute in 1979 and has about 30 years of experience as a specialist in flexibility and supports of critical piping systems for the process industry. During his career back in 1979 he was piping stress and flexibility specialist, later office head of piping flexibility, coordinator and representative of the IMP in the Laguna Verde project (a nuclear power plant in Mexico). He was also the pipe stress analysis department head from 1994–1998. Currently he is a researcher in the applications of pipe stress analysis. He has deep practical experience in pipe stress analysis for nuclear power projects, for process and power plants involving all the different phases of engineering projects, from engineering design to plants start up and operation.
